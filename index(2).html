Core principles

Serious enough for public education and institutional contact, but culturally distinct enough to feel alive, legible, and collective.

Practical autonomy

Teach repeatable skills that reduce dependence on opaque defaults and make people more capable in everyday technical life.

Mutual learning

Build shared literacy through workshops, field guides, and collaborative practice instead of expert gatekeeping.

Humane self-protection

Frame security and privacy as dignity, care, and empowerment — not paranoia, spectacle, or purity tests.

What this could offer

One homepage can hold teaching, publishing, and support structures without losing clarity, as long as the tone stays disciplined and service-oriented.

Workshops

Short, approachable sessions on privacy basics, operational habits, protective tooling, and collective skill-building.

Field guides

Print-friendly, calm reference material with clear steps, short checklists, and practical next actions.

Mutual aid support

Small interventions for people and groups who need protective infrastructure, clearer defaults, or confident technical accompaniment.
